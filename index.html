About Us

PSG College Of Technology

PSG College of Technology, a government-aided institution affiliated to Anna University, stands as a beacon of engineering education since its establishment in 1951. With ISO 9001:2015 certification and a vision to lead in education, research, and societal impact, PSG Tech is committed to providing world-class engineering education, promoting research and development, fostering innovative technology applications, encouraging entrepreneurship, and shaping leaders for the betterment of the country.

CSEA

The Computer Science and Engineering Association (CSEA) is the disciplinary association of the Department of Computer Science and Engineering, dedicated to upskilling CSE students through various events and workshops. Serving as a platform to enhance practical knowledge and technical skills, CSEA organizes group discussions, seminars, workshops, guest lectures, and competitions. It aims to foster healthy interactions among students of different years, engaging them in technical discussions and competitive events. Students benefit from the association by showcasing both technical and non technical talents, helping them discover their passion. At CSEA, we make a difference by assisting students in benefiting from the association, and we look forward to continuing this support in all future endeavors.

CSE Department

The Department of Computer Science and Engineering, propelled by a passionate faculty and driven students, is dedicated to achieving global excellence in education and research. Bolstered by strong partnerships and substantial grants from global organizations, it actively collaborates with academia, government, and industry leaders. Aspiring to be a global leader, the department focuses on producing top-tier graduates, fostering technical proficiency, and conducting impactful research that tackles real-world challenges through the lens of emerging technologies and social consciousness.

FAQ

What is hackathon?

A hackathon is an intensive, time-bound event where participants collaborate to develop innovative solutions or projects, usually related to technology and software, aiming to address a specific challenge or problem. It promotes creativity, collaboration, and rapid prototyping, culminating in presentations to judges and potential recognition or prizes for the most compelling solutions.

Who can participate?

The hackathon is open to all students interested in technology, coding, and innovation. Participants can be from any field of study.

How can I register for the hackathon?

Participants can register on the official website of the CSEA or through the designated registration platform. Follow the registration instructions provided on the event's webpage.

What should I bring to the hackathon?

Participants are required to bring their laptops, any specific hardware they might need, and any other tools or resources necessary for their project. Additionally, it's recommended to bring along any personal items they might need during the event.

What are the judging criteria?

The projects will be evaluated based on criteria such as creativity, technical difficulty, execution, impact, and presentation. Detailed judging criteria will be provided at the start of the event.

Can I start working on my project before the event?

No, all projects must be started and completed during the duration of the hackathon. Any pre-developed projects or code will not be eligible for submission.

What are the prizes for the winners?

Details about the prizes will be revealed at the event. Prizes may include cash rewards, sponsored tech gadgets, internship opportunities, and more.
